# AI Daily · 2026-07-18

> 发布日期:2026-07-18 · 类型:AI 热点日报

---

The strongest signal today is that AI is moving from a model-capability race into a work-system race. Coding benchmarks, search agents, open weights, reasoning harnesses, realtime media generation, and cost-aware scorecards are all circling the same question: can AI keep improving inside real tasks while making the human operator stronger?

**1. Claude sets a stronger coding-tool signal on CursorBench**  
Cursor-related evaluation notes indicate that Claude Fable 5 reached 72.9% on CursorBench, setting a new high for AI coding performance inside realistic editor workflows. Why it matters: coding ability is no longer just about producing a plausible function. The useful frontier is whether a model can understand project context, survive evaluation, fit into the edit-review-test loop, and help a developer compound judgment over time. For readers, the practical lesson is to make their own work easier to evaluate, roll back, and review. [source](https://claude.com/blog/working-at-the-frontier-cursor)

**2. Kimi K2.5 puts Agent Swarm into the foundation layer**  
Moonshot AI disclosed a Kimi K2.5 technical direction that includes MuonClip, linear attention, and Agent Swarm, pointing toward deeper changes in model architecture, memory, and coordinated execution. Why it matters: agents are not just chat interfaces added at the application layer. If multiple intelligent units can be organized, routed, and improved through feedback, the system starts to resemble a self-evolving machine rather than a one-shot answer generator. That is the more important shift to watch. [source](https://x.com/dotey/status/2078172517085085951)

**3. Kimi K3 tops a frontend coding leaderboard with open weights**  
Kimi K3 reportedly reached the top of a frontend coding leaderboard while using an open-weight strategy to challenge the advantage of closed models. Why it matters: frontend coding sits close to everyday product delivery, where small changes quickly become visible user experience. Open weights also give teams a chance to connect models to local workflows, private data, custom evaluation, and deployment constraints. The opportunity is not merely to swap models; it is to turn a model into owned engineering infrastructure. [source](https://x.com/AYi_AInotes/status/2077981025905316253)

**4. OpenAI proposes “useful intelligence per dollar” as an AI scorecard**  
OpenAI framed “useful intelligence per dollar” as a way to measure AI value in real work, shifting attention beyond leaderboard scores toward cost, throughput, and practical usefulness. Why it matters: this is a healthier lens for builders. A model that looks strongest in isolation may not be the best choice once latency, failure rate, token cost, review effort, and integration burden are counted. The next mature AI stack will be judged by total work produced per unit of cost, not just by peak benchmark numbers. [source](https://openai.com/index/a-scorecard-for-the-ai-age/)

**5. Meituan LongCat releases LoHoSearch for harder search-agent evaluation**  
LoHoSearch is positioned as a more difficult benchmark for search agents, requiring systems to find, judge, and integrate information in complex search environments. Why it matters: the future of agents is not “the model can browse the web.” The hard part is building an evidence trail across incomplete, conflicting, and noisy sources. Search, reasoning, citation, caching, and review need to become one loop. That loop is what turns an agent from a confident narrator into a useful research worker. [source](https://x.com/Meituan_LongCat/status/2078119654632124547)

**6. Schema Harness reports about 99% on the ARC-AGI-3 public set**  
Schema Harness reported roughly 99% performance on the ARC-AGI-3 public set, bringing structured problem-solving frameworks and general reasoning evaluation back into focus. Why it matters: AI progress does not only come from larger models. It also comes from better task representation, validation, and feedback. When a problem can be shaped into a structure that a machine can test and improve against, human thinking gains a new lever: design the loop, not just the prompt. [source](https://schema-harness.github.io/)

**7. Tongyi Wan-Streamer v0.2 pushes video generation toward realtime interaction**  
Tongyi Lab released Wan-Streamer v0.2, emphasizing end-to-end response latency around 550ms and pushing video generation closer to interactive use. Why it matters: video AI becomes more interesting when it stops being a slow render box and starts becoming a responsive creative instrument. When feedback is fast enough, the creator can think, revise, and generate in the same flow. That is the difference between AI as an output machine and AI as an extension of human expression. [source](https://mp.weixin.qq.com/s/_eaO0wmsiQFGsrE2_zW_Dg)

**Today's takeaway:** The next AI advantage will not belong only to people who know which model is strongest. It will belong to people who can turn models, evaluation, evidence, cost, and feedback into a working system. The question worth asking: which repeated workflow in your life could become an AI loop that leaves evidence, corrects itself, and makes you stronger?
