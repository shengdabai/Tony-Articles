# The Constraint Is the Creator

> 发布日期:2026-06-23 · [中文](../zh/2026-06-23-约束才是创作者.md) | [English](../en/2026-06-23-the-constraint-is-the-creator.md)

---

AI gives people a dangerous feeling:

Maybe I can finally create without limits.

No shortage of words. No shortage of images. No shortage of research material. No shortage of outlines, drafts, summaries, code, designs, translations, titles, scripts, and variations. The blank page is no longer blank for long. The machine can fill it before your hesitation has time to become embarrassing.

At first, this feels like freedom.

But after using AI seriously for a while, I keep arriving at the opposite conclusion.

The problem of the AI era is not that we have too many limits. The problem is that we have too few meaningful limits.

When output becomes cheap, the old fantasy of total freedom becomes less useful. A person with no constraint can generate endless material, but endless material is not a work. A work needs shape. Shape comes from exclusion. And exclusion is a kind of constraint.

So the uncomfortable truth is this:

In the age of AI, the constraint is not the enemy of the creator.

The constraint is the creator.

## Freedom Without Shape Becomes Noise

We often talk about creativity as if it means removing boundaries.

Give me more time. More tools. More references. More models. More memory. More possibilities. Then I will create something better.

Sometimes that is true. A person who lacks basic tools does need better tools. A person trapped by meaningless labor does need relief. AI is excellent at removing dead friction: cleaning notes, finding examples, translating drafts, formatting files, checking obvious errors, and turning rough material into something easier to inspect.

But there is another kind of friction that should not be removed too quickly.

Choosing the point.

Deciding what this piece is not about.

Holding one line of logic long enough for it to become alive.

Refusing a beautiful paragraph because it weakens the structure.

Admitting that a clever sentence is only decoration.

These are not low-value obstacles. They are part of the creative act.

The machine can produce more options than we can read. That means abundance is no longer impressive by itself. The person who can generate twenty drafts is not rare. The person who can delete nineteen for the right reason is rare.

This is why many AI-generated works feel strangely weightless. They are not always bad at the sentence level. In fact, the sentences may be smooth. The problem is that nothing had to be sacrificed. No pressure shaped the piece. No internal law made one choice necessary and another choice impossible.

Without a frame, freedom does not become art.

It becomes fog.

## Constraint Is How Attention Becomes Form

There is a reason many creative practices depend on rules.

A sonnet is not just a poem with fewer options. Jazz improvisation is not just musical freedom. A good game is not interesting because the player can do everything. It is interesting because the player can do something meaningful inside a designed world of limits.

The rules do not merely restrict movement. They make movement visible.

Research on creativity and innovation says something similar. In a 2018 cross-disciplinary review, Oguz Acar, Murat Tarakci, and Daan van Knippenberg synthesized work across fields and argued that constraints can either hinder or help creativity depending on type and intensity. Their model suggests an inverted U-shaped relationship: too few constraints can leave people unfocused, while too many can suffocate exploration.[^1]

That fits lived experience.

No constraint says: do anything.

Too much constraint says: do only this.

A useful constraint says: move here, but make it yours.

One famous example is *Green Eggs and Ham*. The story is often traced to a challenge that Theodor Geisel, better known as Dr. Seuss, write a children's book using only fifty different words.[^2] The limit did not make the book small. It gave the book its engine: repetition, rhythm, insistence, and play.

The same pattern appears in many serious crafts.

A photographer chooses one lens for a walk.

A writer gives herself a fixed word count.

A programmer restricts an experiment to one file.

A teacher explains a hard idea without jargon.

A founder builds only for one narrow customer before expanding.

The limit is not there to make the person less capable. It is there to concentrate capability.

Attention is weak when it is spread across everything.

Attention becomes force when it has a wall to push against.

## AI Removes the Wrong Excuse

Before AI, many people had a convenient excuse:

I would create more if I had better tools.

That excuse is getting weaker every month.

The tools are here. Not perfect, but already powerful enough to remove a large amount of mechanical difficulty. You can ask for research leads, structure options, critique, examples, editing, translation, diagrams, code, and tests. You can work in a second language. You can prototype without being an expert in every layer.

This is wonderful.

It is also exposing.

Because once the tool bottleneck falls, the real bottleneck becomes visible:

What do you actually want to make?

What standard will you hold?

What will you refuse?

What kind of person is allowed to appear through the work?

AI can help with a draft, but it cannot care on your behalf. It can offer ten directions, but it cannot make one direction morally expensive for you. It can imitate a style, but it cannot decide what your life has trained you to notice.

That is why "human creativity" should not be romanticized as some mystical cloud. It is often much more ordinary and much harder.

It is the accumulated ability to choose constraints that reveal your taste.

The person who says, "Make it good," has almost said nothing.

The person who says, "Make it useful for a beginner, but do not flatter them; make it warm, but not soft; make it practical, but leave one sentence that changes how they see the problem," has already begun creating before the first sentence is written.

The constraint carries the worldview.

## The Best AI Use Is Not Unlimited Generation

This changes how I think about using AI.

The shallow use of AI is asking it to remove all pressure:

Write anything.

Give me ideas.

Make this better.

Expand this.

Do the whole thing.

Those requests are not useless. They can start motion. But if we stay there, AI becomes a machine for avoiding the very decisions that make us stronger.

A deeper use is to ask AI to help build and test constraints:

What is the one claim this essay must defend?

Which paragraph is outside the frame?

What private detail should be abstracted before publishing?

What assumption would make this argument collapse?

Can this be explained without name-dropping, jargon, or borrowed authority?

Where does this sound polished but not earned?

This is not slower in the long run. It is faster in the way compound interest is faster: small discipline now prevents expensive confusion later.

AI is very good at producing material. But a creator's job is not to become a passive consumer of machine material. The job is to create a field of force: a problem, a rule, a taste, a standard, a boundary, a feedback loop.

Inside that field, the machine becomes useful.

Outside that field, the machine becomes a fog machine.

## A Personal Constraint Is a Personal Philosophy

There is one more reason constraints matter.

They reveal who you are.

Anyone can say they value learning. But the person who writes a note only after testing an idea has a different philosophy from the person who collects summaries all day.

Anyone can say they value quality. But the person who deletes a popular line because it is inaccurate has a different philosophy from the person who keeps it because it sounds good.

Anyone can say they want AI to make people stronger. But the person who designs AI workflows with verification, privacy, memory, and deliberate practice has a different philosophy from the person who uses AI only to avoid effort.

Your constraints are your values made operational.

This is why self-imposed limits are so powerful. They are not merely productivity tricks. They are a way of turning vague ideals into behavior.

For writing, a constraint might be:

I will only publish what can survive privacy review.

I will not use a fact I cannot source.

I will write for ordinary people without lowering the intellectual standard.

I will let AI help with language, but not outsource the central judgment.

For learning, a constraint might be:

I will predict before I ask for the answer.

I will explain before I collect another explanation.

I will build a small artifact before I declare that I understand.

For work, a constraint might be:

I will automate repeated labor, but not automate responsibility.

I will make the next repetition easier, not only finish today's task.

I will not call something done until reality has touched it.

These constraints look small. But small constraints, repeated, become identity.

## The New Freedom Is Chosen Difficulty

The AI era does not remove the need for human effort.

It changes the location of effort.

Less effort should go into mechanical production. More effort should go into framing, judging, testing, connecting, caring, and refusing.

This is good news, but only for people willing to grow up with the tools.

If we use AI to escape all difficulty, we become softer. We produce more, but understand less. We move faster, but choose worse. We collect outputs, but lose the muscle that gives output meaning.

If we use AI to choose better difficulty, we become stronger.

We can make the boring parts cheaper while making the important parts more visible.

We can turn a vague wish into a rule.

We can turn a repeated mistake into a checklist.

We can turn scattered notes into a living argument.

We can turn unlimited generation into deliberate creation.

That, to me, is the real promise.

Not a world where machines create and humans watch.

Not a world where humans pretend to be more original than they are.

But a world where a person can build sharper constraints, use stronger tools, and slowly become more worthy of the freedom those tools provide.

Because freedom is not the absence of limits.

Freedom is the ability to choose the limits that make you larger.
